# Debate Dictionary

---
## 1. VERBS: Assert, Propose & State

### Assert
- **Español:** Afirmar (con autoridad), aseverar
- **Meaning:** To state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*asserted*)
- **Examples:** "The author **asserts** that moral values are objective."

### Contend
- **Español:** Sostener (un argumento); lidiar con
- **Meaning:** To assert a position in an argument; to struggle with difficulty
- **Grammar:** [Tr/Intr] [Reg] (*contended*)
- **Examples:** "Critics **contend** that this law infringes upon personal liberties."

### Maintain
- **Español:** Mantener, sostener (ante la duda)
- **Meaning:** To state strongly that something is true, especially when doubted
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*maintained*)
- **Examples:** "Despite the evidence, he **maintains** his innocence."

### Posit
- **Español:** Postular, plantear
- **Meaning:** To assume as a fact; to put forward as a basis of argument
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*posited*)
- **Examples:** "Let us **posit** that free will is an illusion."

### Purport
- **Español:** Pretender (ser algo que no es)
- **Meaning:** To appear or claim to be or do something, especially falsely
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*purported*)
- **Examples:** "This document **purports** to be official, but lacks a signature."

### Aver
- **Español:** Aseverar (formal/legal)
- **Meaning:** To state or assert to be the case
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*averred*)
- **Examples:** "The witness **averred** that he saw the defendant."

### Profess
- **Español:** Profesar, declarar abiertamente
- **Meaning:** To claim that one has (a quality/feeling); to affirm faith
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*professed*)
- **Examples:** "He **professes** to be an expert, yet understands nothing."

### Allege
- **Español:** Alegar (sin pruebas aún)
- **Meaning:** To claim someone has done something wrong without proof
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*alleged*)
- **Examples:** "The prosecution **alleges** that funds were embezzled."

### Avow
- **Español:** Confesar, reconocer francamente
- **Meaning:** To assert or confess openly
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*avowed*)
- **Examples:** "He is an **avowed** pacifist."

### Proclaim
- **Español:** Proclamar
- **Meaning:** To announce officially or publicly
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*proclaimed*)
- **Examples:** "The government **proclaimed** a state of emergency."

### Enunciate
- **Español:** Enunciar, articular
- **Meaning:** To say clearly; to express a theory in definite terms
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*enunciated*)
- **Examples:** "The CEO **enunciated** the new strategic vision."

### Articulate
- **Español:** Articular (ideas complejas)
- **Meaning:** To express fluently and coherently
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*articulated*)
- **Examples:** "She struggled to **articulate** her feelings."

### Voice
- **Español:** Expresar, dar voz
- **Meaning:** To express (something) in words
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*voiced*)
- **Examples:** "Employees **voiced** their concerns."

### Propound
- **Español:** Proponer (teoría/idea) para consideración
- **Meaning:** To put forward for consideration by others
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*propounded*)
- **Examples:** "Darwin **propounded** the theory of natural selection."

### Advance
- **Español:** Avanzar (un argumento)
- **Meaning:** To put forward (a theory or suggestion)
- **Grammar:** [Tr] [Reg] (*advanced*)

## 4. NOUNS: Arguments & Logical Concepts

### Premise
- **Español:** Premisa
- **Meaning:** A previous statement from which another is inferred as conclusion
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "Your argument rests on the false **premise** that users read manuals."

### Contention
- **Español:** Contención (punto de disputa)
- **Meaning:** An assertion maintained in argument; a point of disagreement
- **Grammar:** Noun [C/U]
- **Examples:** "It is my **contention** that microservices will increase costs."

### Assertion
- **Español:** Aseveración, afirmación rotunda
- **Meaning:** A confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "I challenge your **assertion** that this code is ready."

### Proposition
- **Español:** Proposición, propuesta lógica
- **Meaning:** A statement that expresses a judgment or opinion
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "The **proposition** to switch to a 4-day work week was debated."

### Thesis
- **Español:** Tesis (idea central a probar)
- **Meaning:** A statement put forward as a premise to be proved
- **Grammar:** Noun [C] Plural: *Theses*
- **Examples:** "The central **thesis** is that technology drives social change."

### Hypothesis
- **Español:** Hipótesis
- **Meaning:** A supposition made as a starting point for investigation
- **Grammar:** Noun [C] Plural: *Hypotheses*
- **Examples:** "We are testing the **hypothesis** that the new UI improves retention."

### Postulate
- **Español:** Postulado
- **Meaning:** A thing assumed as true as the basis for reasoning
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "Euclid's parallel **postulate** is the foundation of geometry."

### Conjecture
- **Español:** Conjetura
- **Meaning:** An opinion formed on incomplete information; a guess
- **Grammar:** Noun [C/U]
- **Examples:** "Without logs, the cause is pure **conjecture**."

### Supposition
- **Español:** Suposición
- **Meaning:** An uncertain belief; an idea that something may be true
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "Your argument is based on the **supposition** that the market will grow."

### Surmise
- **Español:** Sospecha, conjetura (leve)
- **Meaning:** A supposition without evidence to confirm it
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "My **surmise** is that the database lock is causing the timeout."

### Inference
- **Español:** Inferencia
- **Meaning:** A conclusion reached on the basis of evidence and reasoning
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "We can draw an **inference** about the user's location."

### Deduction
- **Español:** Deducción (de lo general a específico)
- **Meaning:** The inference of particular instances from a general law
- **Grammar:** Noun [C/U]
- **Examples:** "Through logical **deduction**, we identified the memory leak."

### Induction
- **Español:** Inducción (de lo específico a general)
- **Meaning:** The inference of a general law from particular instances
- **Grammar:** Noun [Uncountable]
- **Examples:** "Scientific laws are established through **induction**."

### Corollary
- **Español:** Corolario
- **Meaning:** A proposition that follows from one already proved
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "Loss of privacy is a **corollary** of digital convenience."

### Paradox
- **Español:** Paradoja
- **Meaning:** A seemingly absurd statement that may prove well founded
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "The **paradox**: we have more data but less insight."

### Fallacy
- **Español:** Falacia
- **Meaning:** A mistaken belief based on unsound argument
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "The 'sunk cost **fallacy**' keeps people investing in failing projects."

### Anomaly
- **Español:** Anomalía
- **Meaning:** Something that deviates from what is standard or expected
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "We detected a statistical **anomaly** in the traffic data."

### Phenomenon
- **Español:** Fenómeno
- **Meaning:** A fact or situation that is observed to exist or happen
- **Grammar:** Noun [C] Plural: *Phenomena*
- **Examples:** "The placebo effect is a psychological **phenomenon**."

### Paradigm
- **Español:** Paradigma
- **Meaning:** A typical example or pattern; a model or framework
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "We need a **paradigm** shift in how we approach this."

### Axiom
- **Español:** Axioma
- **Meaning:** A statement regarded as self-evidently true
- **Grammar:** Noun [Countable]
- **Examples:** "You cannot argue against a mathematical **axiom**."
