## Self-Review Checklist
- [ ] Both sides are presented fairly and respectfully
- [ ] Arguments are supported with evidence, not just opinions
- [ ] Doesn't strawman opposing views
- [ ] Shows understanding of complexity/nuance
- [ ] Transitions smoothly between viewpoints
- [ ] Conclusion is balanced, not one-sided
- [ ] Acknowledges that reasonable people can disagree
- [ ] Formal, objective tone throughout

## Balanced Language
| Presenting views | Transitioning | Concluding |
|------------------|---------------|------------|
| Proponents argue... | On the other hand,... | On balance,... |
| Supporters contend... | Conversely,... | Taking all factors into account,... |
| Those in favor claim... | However,... | Weighing the evidence,... |
| Critics maintain... | Nevertheless,... | It appears that,... |
| Opponents point out... | Despite this,... | A nuanced view suggests... |

## Hedging Language (for balanced conclusions)
- "It seems reasonable to suggest that..."
- "The evidence tends to indicate..."
- "While there is no definitive answer..."
- "This may depend on..."
- "In certain circumstances..."
- "There is merit to both perspectives..."
